# Course: Supporting a Friend With Screen Overuse (Without Becoming Their Coach)  
**Persona:** supporter  
**Level:** intro  

---

## 1. Course Overview

**Goal:**  
Help friends support someone whose screen use worries them, without turning the friendship into a project or losing themselves in the process.

**Outcomes:**

- Understand what screens may be doing “for” the friend, not only “to” them.
- Start one low-pressure, honest conversation.
- Set and hold a simple friendship boundary.
- Know when to encourage additional support.

**Gamification:**

- Badges:
  - M1: “Context Finder”
  - M2: “Gentle Starter”
  - M3: “Steady Friend”
  - M4: “Support Scout”
- “Check-in quests”:
  - Short, low-stakes conversations that protect the friendship.

---

## 2. Module 1 – Understanding Your Friend's Screen World  
**ID:** c4-m1  
**Access:** free  

Lessons:

1. **Lesson 1.1 – What you notice vs what they experience**
   - You notice:
     - canceled plans, short replies, late-night sessions.
   - They might experience:
     - relief, connection, focus, escape.
   - Simple two-column printable.

2. **Lesson 1.2 – Needs screens may be meeting**
   - Escape from stress or loneliness.
   - Feeling competent or successful.
   - Belonging or community.
   - Predictable comfort.
   - Exercise: list 3 possible needs for your friend.

3. **Lesson 1.3 – Care vs fixing**
   - Definitions with simple examples.
   - Reflection sheet:
     - “I care because…”
     - “I feel responsible when…”

4. **Lesson 1.4 – Your friendship matters too**
   - Validate grief, frustration, and distance.
   - You can care and still have limits.
   - Exercise: 3 things you want to protect in the friendship.

5. **Challenge:**  
   - Write two lists:
     - “What screens are doing to them.”
     - “What screens are doing for them.”

---

## 3. Module 2 – Conversations That Stay Light and Honest  
**ID:** c4-m2  

Lessons:

1. **Right moment, right mood.**
   - Choose low-stress times.
2. **Gentle openers.**
   - Simple, curious scripts and “I statements.”
3. **Listening without fixing.**
   - Reflect back, pause, and ask open questions.
4. **Common friendship traps.**
   - Avoid jokes that sting, nagging, or public call-outs.
5. **Challenge:** one low-stakes check-in using a script.

---

## 4. Module 3 – Friendship Boundaries and Self-Respect  
**ID:** c4-m3  

Lessons:

1. **Boundary vs ultimatum.**
2. **Deciding what you can offer.**
3. **Saying no without guilt.**
4. **Protecting your routines.**
5. **Challenge:** define and share one clear boundary.

---

## 5. Module 4 – When to Encourage More Support  
**ID:** c4-m4  

Lessons:

1. **Signals that more support may be needed.**
2. **How to suggest help without shaming.**
3. **Support options map (trusted people, professional, community).**
4. **Your own support.**
5. **Final challenge:** list 3 support options and one action step for yourself.

# Course: Supporting a Loved One With Screen Overuse (Without Losing Yourself)  
**Persona:** supporter  
**Level:** intro  

---

## 1. Course Overview

**Goal:**  
Help partners, parents, and friends support someone whose screen use worries them, without burning out or turning every talk into a fight.

**Outcomes:**

- Understand what screens may be doing “for” the other person, not only “to” them.
- Start more effective conversations.
- Set and hold personal boundaries.
- Know when and how to suggest external help.

**Gamification:**

- Badges:
  - M1: “Observer”
  - M2: “Bridge Builder”
  - M3: “Boundary Keeper”
  - M4: “Wise Ally”
- “Conversation quests”:
  - Small, structured, low-risk talks.

---

## 2. Module 1 – Understanding What Your Loved One Gets From Screens  
**ID:** c3-m1  
**Access:** free  

Lessons:

1. **Lesson 1.1 – What you see vs what they feel**
   - You see:
     - distance, silence, late nights.
   - They feel:
     - safe, competent, connected, distracted.
   - Simple two-column printable.

2. **Lesson 1.2 – Needs screens may be meeting**
   - Escape from stress or shame.
   - Feeling powerful or competent.
   - Social belonging and community.
   - Predictable comfort.
   - Exercise: list 3 possible needs for your loved one.

3. **Lesson 1.3 – Concern vs control**
   - Definitions with simple examples.
   - Reflection sheet:
     - “I worry because…”
     - “I want them to change so that…”

4. **Lesson 1.4 – Your feelings also matter**
   - Validate exhaustion, anger, sadness.
   - You can care and still set limits.
   - Exercise: 3 things you need to protect for yourself.

5. **Challenge:**  
   - Write two lists:
     - “What screens are doing to them.”
     - “What screens are doing for them.”

---

## 3. Module 2 – Conversations That Open Doors  
**ID:** c3-m2  

Lessons:

1. **When not to talk.**
   - Avoid during active conflict or during intense use.
2. **How to start a gentle conversation.**
   - Script templates: “I statements”, no accusations.
3. **Listening skills.**
   - Reflect back, ask open questions.
4. **What to avoid.**
   - threats, lectures, sarcasm.
5. **Challenge:** one short, planned conversation using a script.

---

## 4. Module 3 – Boundaries, Limits, and Self-Protection  
**ID:** c3-m3  

Lessons:

1. **Difference between boundaries and control.**
2. **Deciding what you can and cannot tolerate.**
3. **Communicating consequences without drama.**
4. **Protecting your energy and time.**
5. **Challenge:** write and share one boundary.

---

## 5. Module 4 – When and How to Seek External Help  
**ID:** c3-m4  

Lessons:

1. **Red flags that may require professional support.**
2. **How to talk about help without shaming.**
3. **Different types of support (family, medical, therapeutic, community).**
4. **Taking care of yourself while they decide.**
5. **Final challenge:** list 3 support options and one action step for yourself.

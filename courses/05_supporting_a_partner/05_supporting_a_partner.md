# Course: Supporting a Partner With Screen Overuse (Without Becoming the Screen Police)  
**Persona:** supporter  
**Level:** intro  

---

## 1. Course Overview

**Goal:**  
Help partners support someone whose screen use is hurting connection, without turning the relationship into a constant conflict.

**Outcomes:**

- Understand what screens may be doing “for” the partner, not only “to” them.
- Start one calm, connection-focused conversation.
- Set and hold one clear relationship boundary or agreement.
- Know when to encourage additional support.

**Gamification:**

- Badges:
  - M1: “Empathy Reader”
  - M2: “Repair Starter”
  - M3: “Boundary Partner”
  - M4: “Support Pathfinder”
- “Repair quests”:
  - Small, connection-first talks that reduce tension.

---

## 2. Module 1 – Understanding Your Partner's Screen World  
**ID:** c5-m1  
**Access:** free  

Lessons:

1. **Lesson 1.1 – What you experience vs what they experience**
   - You experience:
     - bedtime distance, short replies, and divided attention.
   - They might experience:
     - relief, focus, connection, escape.
   - Simple two-column printable.

2. **Lesson 1.2 – Needs screens may be meeting**
   - Escape from stress or pressure.
   - Feeling competent or successful.
   - Belonging or community.
   - Predictable comfort.
   - Exercise: list 3 possible needs for your partner.

3. **Lesson 1.3 – Care vs control**
   - Definitions with simple examples.
   - Reflection sheet:
     - “I care because…”
     - “I try to control when…”

4. **Lesson 1.4 – Your relationship matters too**
   - Validate loneliness, resentment, and distance.
   - You can care and still have limits.
   - Exercise: 3 things you want to protect in the relationship.

5. **Challenge:**  
   - Write two lists:
     - “What screens are doing to them.”
     - “What screens are doing for them.”

---

## 3. Module 2 – Conversations That Repair Connection  
**ID:** c5-m2  

Lessons:

1. **Right moment, right tone.**
   - Choose low-stress times and softer tone.
2. **Connection-first openers.**
   - Use “I statements” and appreciation before concerns.
3. **Listening for what matters.**
   - Reflect back needs, not just words.
4. **Patterns that escalate.**
   - Avoid sarcasm, scorekeeping, and urgent ultimatums.
5. **Challenge:** one short repair conversation with one small request.

---

## 4. Module 3 – Agreements, Boundaries, and Self-Respect  
**ID:** c5-m3  

Lessons:

1. **Boundary vs rule.**
2. **Deciding what you need.**
3. **Making shared agreements.**
4. **Protecting your routines.**
5. **Challenge:** draft one shared agreement and revisit it after a week.

---

## 5. Module 4 – When to Encourage More Support  
**ID:** c5-m4  

Lessons:

1. **Signals that more support may be needed.**
2. **How to suggest help without shaming.**
3. **Support options map (trusted people, professional, community).**
4. **Your own support.**
5. **Final challenge:** list 3 support options and one action step for yourself.
